UNHRD's 2018 in review

2018 was another challenging year for the humanitarian community.
As new crises occur now and then, some existing ones are protracting, some others are becoming complex, this has unfortunately become a default statement over the past decade.

Foreword

In June we marked the 18th birthday of UNHRD, celebrating the establishment in 2000 of our first hub in Brindisi. In many countries of the world turning 18 means becoming adult, now more than ever we have to confirm that we have grown as a vital tool for emergency preparedness and response, be accountable for our job and reinforce that humanitarian partners can rely on us to meet more, multiple and demanding challenges.

This Year in Review provides fact and figures that clearly quantify the support we provided to our partners to respond to crisis all over the world. It didn’t take much to gather these figures as we are now testing and starting using automated reporting tools, which we will make soon available to our partners as part of our efforts to also expand our digital services. Through our revamped website and new platform, we aim at allowing organizations working with us have full control of their stocks analytics and activities reports.

What cannot be automatically and directly quantified is the work behind the scenes that makes every operation possible. From the suppliers to the final destinations, there a number of important back-office functions to deliver relief items and logistics equipment. We are dedicating some space in this report to recognize the work of staff who provide fundamental support in procurement, finance, human resources and admin making the UNHRD mechanism up and running, smoothly and efficiently.

Their job is also crucial to deploy the UNHRD Rapid Response Team or other staff for temporary duty assignments to operations. During 2018, we put even more efforts to provide technical support to our colleagues in the field. Similarly, we have organized, participated to or hosted trainings and simulations to build logistics and response capacities of our partners. Out of the many of us who travelled to XX countries, this brief recalls the experience of colleagues who were deployed to XX, XX and XX, and reports of capacity building activities in XX, XX and XX.

As we try to keep up with new challenges and contribute more and more to the Sustainable Development Goals, since a few years UNHRD has its own innovation Lab. Hosted in our Brindisi hub, it is a research and development unit that engages in efforts to improve logistics support equipment and packaging solutions. Partnering with humanitarian organizations, academia, private sectors and other stakeholders we are committed to help the humanitarian community find new ways to reduce the environmental impact of operations including by integrating green technologies, reducing waste and reusing materials. We usually say, and we said to many curious colleagues during the last Global Partners Meeting: hard to understand what it is about, until you see it yourself! For 2019 and beyond – in addition to perform and be accountable for our core business – we are committed to produce a regular newsletter on the Lab’s projects and on other major updates.

Once again, and this is not a default statement, we would like to thank our donors for their generous support and our partners and colleagues for their teamwork. This is what we call ‘working together’, what allow us to reach people in need and save lives.

Yemen

In 2018, the Yemen crisis has hit the headlines as the world’s worst humanitarian crisis. Since March 2015, the conflict has exacerbated the already precarious situation in Yemen – the poorest country in the Arab world since before the war. Against this backdrop, Yemen also faced the worst cholera outbreak in 2017 with more than one million suspected cases reported by the end of December and a new acceleration in September 2018. UNHRD has supported partner organizations in their logistics and relief efforts especially by dispatching prefabricated mobile tents to increase the warehousing capacity for the humanitarian community operating in the country.

Bangladesh

Described as the fastest growing and one of the largest refugee crisis in the world, the Rohingya emergency in Bangladesh has entered its second year in August 2018. More than 900,000 refugees live in the area of Cox’s Bazaar, with Kutupalong-Balukhali camp as the largest and most densely populated refugee camp in the world. The vast majority of them rely on humanitarian assistance to survive. Throughout the year, one of the main challenges has been the safety of the camps during the Monsoon season. Out of 449 tons of equipment and aid delivered by UNHRD to Bangladesh, 53.65 % or 241 tons consisted of emergency shelter.

Democratic Republic of Congo

With 13.1 million people in dire need of assistance, the Democratic Republic of the Congo continues to witness one of the most multiform and long-standing humanitarian crisis. The situation has further deteriorated with an outbreak of cholera in the North Kivu, officially declared in August 2018. This can be described as the most complex Ebola outbreak response in history, with the virus found in an area where there is a violent conflict and due to record numbers of refugees and internally displaced people. UNHRD has supported aid organizations operating in such exceptionally complex and challenging environment to prevent and control the spread of the disease.

Indonesia

On 28 September a number of strong earthquakes hit Central Sulawesi in Indonesia with a 7.4 magnitude shock triggering a tsunami that struck Palu City. The response has been led by the Government of Indonesia, through the National Disaster Management Authority (BNPB), in collaboration with the ASEAN Coordinating Centre for Humanitarian Assistance on Disaster Management (AHA Centre). They welcomed offers of international assistance as of 1 October to meet specific humanitarian needs. Since then, UNHRD has supported partners delivering much needed emergency shelter, water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH) items and logistics equipment.

A devastating 7.5 magnitude earthquake followed by a series of severe aftershocks caused widespread damages in Papua New Guinea leaving many villages isolated and more than 270.000 people in need of humanitarian assistance.

UNHRD airlifted 80 tons of high-energy biscuits for WFP to assist 60.000 people affected by the earthquake and three prefabricated storage units to set up a staging area. The airlift was facilitated by the International Humanitarian City (IHC) through a plane provided by His Highness Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice-President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai.

Uganda

Uganda hosts more refugees than any other country in Africa, including people who have fled from South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of Congo and Burundi. In addition to supporting over one million refugees as well as Ugandans affected by recurring climate shocks with food assistance, WFP provides other humanitarian agencies with supply chain services and expertise to support their operations in country and in the region.

In March, a B747 carried 30 prefabricated storage tents, 30 generators and 5 scales to Entebbe for WFP’s refugee response. Out of a total 516.2 MT of cargo shipped to Uganda through 30 deliveries for WFP, WHO and FAO, 87.8 % were logistics equipment.

Niger

The humanitarian crisis in Niger arises from vulnerabilities common to the Sahel region related to malnutrition, food insecurity and climate shocks. Conflicts in neighboring countries and epidemics, both internal and external, aggravate the situation.

The Italian Cooperation established an air bridge to Niger with 7 deliveries between April and December to support the health system in the country. A total of 60 metric tons of medicines, health devices and water purification units were airlifted to contribute to the Government’s response to a broad health emergency complicated by meningitis and cholera outbreaks. Combined with deliveries for WHO, medical kits and devices make up the 35,5 % of the 259 MT of cargo delivered to Niger while emergency shelter items represent 47.6 % (IRISH AID?).

Rwanda

A low-income, least-developed country and one of the most densely populated in Africa, Rwanda registers high percentages of population living below the poverty line (44.9 %), undernourishment (41%) and food insecurity (20 %). The current outbreak of Ebola in DR Congo poses a high risk of introduction into neighboring countries including Rwanda which currently hosts 175,000 Congolese and Burundian refugees.

With the support of UPS Foundation, 500 boxes of high-energy biscuits, 5 prefabricated storage units and 2 office modules were delivered to Kigali for WFP’s operations. Throughout 2018, 13 cargos were delivered to Rwanda including medical kits and devices for WHO and laboratory kits for FAO.

Venezuela

The devastating economic crisis hitting Venezuela has driven masses of people to leave the country in search of opportunities. In the country, shortages of food and medicine have led to a crisis in public health. Humanitarian organizations are on the ground in Colombia to assist refugees and in Venezuela to support the population in meeting their basic needs.

USD 5.8 million worth of medicines were delivered to Venezuela for Aid for Aids, one of the most recent partners of UNHRD, who is providing access to medicines to people in need as well as support to local civic organizations.

Partnerships

Global Partners Meeting

Thirty-seven participants from 25 organizations gathered in Rome for the 2018 UNHRD Global Partner Meeting, representing UN agencies, governmental and non-governmental organizations. This was an important opportunity to engage in an open and constructive discussion with partners, in particular to follow up on the action points from 2017 GPM, review customer satisfaction and explain changes made on the Standard Operating Procedure. Specific emphasis was put on the newly-implemented digital platform which will allow partners to monitor their stocks and operations.

Group of Friends of UNHRD

In a ceremony on 18 June at the annual session of the Executive Board of the World Food Programme, the Italian Government handed over the chairmanship of the ‘Group of Friends of UNHRD’ to the Spanish Government. Also promoted by the Government of Panama, the event was the opportunity to celebrate the 18th anniversary of UNHRD, which was first established in Brindisi, Italy in June 2000. Launched in 2015 by the Italian Government, the Group of Friends of UNHRD brings together donors, partners and other actors who aim to raise awareness on the role of UNHRD in the global humanitarian architecture and who recognize the need to invest in emergency preparedness and response to ensure a sustainable UNHRD Network.

New home for UNHRD Panama

In November, Panama’s new Regional Logistics Center for Humanitarian Assistance was officially inaugurated by President Juan Carlos Varela. Consisting of three warehouses for a total storage capacity of 12.000 square meters and cold rooms, the Humanitarian hub hosts UNHRD, the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) and the National Civil Protection System (SINAPROC).
